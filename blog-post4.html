Parent’s Guide to Digital Safety

As children spend more time online for school, entertainment, and learning, it becomes crucial for parents to ensure a safe digital environment. Here are key guidelines to keep in mind:

  • Set clear boundaries for internet use — including time limits and approved websites.
  • Teach children the importance of protecting personal information (like passwords and addresses).
  • Encourage open communication so they feel comfortable reporting suspicious activities.
  • Use parental control tools and monitor browsing activity.
  • Educate them about cyberbullying and how to respond.

10 Tips for Learning to Code

Coding is one of the most valuable skills children can develop. Here are ten practical tips to make the learning process enjoyable and effective:

  1. Start with block-based coding platforms like Scratch before moving to text-based programming.
  2. Break down problems into small steps to build problem-solving skills.
  3. Practice consistently — small daily efforts add up quickly.
  4. Join coding communities to learn from peers and share projects.
  5. Work on real-life mini-projects (like a calculator or simple game).
  6. Explore multiple languages (Python, JavaScript, HTML/CSS) to broaden understanding.
  7. Use online resources like tutorials, courses, and coding challenges.
  8. Encourage curiosity — let kids experiment with code and learn by trial and error.
  9. Celebrate milestones and progress to keep motivation high.
  10. Have fun! Coding is not just about logic; it’s also about creativity.
